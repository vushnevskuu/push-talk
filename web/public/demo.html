How VoiceInsert works

A menu bar utility for macOS: hold your shortcut, speak, release — transcribed text lands in whatever field already has focus (browser, chat, IDE, Obsidian, Notes, etc.). Recognition uses Apple’s on-device Speech framework.

While the shortcut is held

Simplified illustration — the app uses a Metal flame HUD that follows your voice.

One phrase, end to end focus field → hold shortcut → HUD + capture → release → text inserted
  1. First run: permissions & shortcut In VoiceInsert Settings, allow the microphone and speech recognition; grant Accessibility if needed for tricky fields. Pick a global shortcut you can hold comfortably.
  2. Click the target text field The caret should be where you want the transcript — same idea as pasting from the clipboard.
  3. Hold the shortcut and speak A HUD appears along the top edge while audio streams to recognition. It does not steal keyboard focus from your front app.
  4. Release the keys The session ends, the HUD dismisses, and the final text is inserted (paste, typing, or automation depending on the target app).

Second shortcut (optional) — Obsidian capture: your phrase is saved as Markdown in the configured vault under the Voice Captures layout.
